Starting daycare is a big milestone, and a few wobbly mornings are completely normal — for children *and* parents. A little separation anxiety is actually a sign of healthy attachment. The good news: with a calm, consistent approach, most little ones settle in faster than you'd expect. Here's what helps.

## Before the first day

- **Visit together if you can.** Even a short visit to see the room, the toys, and a friendly face makes the new place feel familiar.
- **Talk about it warmly.** Read books about starting daycare and describe the day in simple, happy terms: "You'll play, have a snack, and I'll pick you up after nap."
- **Practice short separations.** Leaving your child with a trusted grown-up for an hour or two builds their confidence that you always come back.

## A goodbye ritual that works

The hardest part is often the drop-off. A predictable, loving routine makes it easier:

- Keep it **short and confident.** A long, anxious goodbye tells your child there's something to worry about.
- Use the same little ritual every day — a hug, a kiss, "see you after nap," and a wave at the window.
- **Always say goodbye.** Sneaking out can feel easier in the moment, but it often makes the next day harder.

## Settling in

- **Send a comfort object** — a favorite stuffed animal or a small blanket from home can be a powerful anchor.
- **Keep your mornings calm** and unhurried; children pick up on our stress.
- **Trust the process.** Many children cry at drop-off and are happily playing minutes later. Your caregivers can tell you how the rest of the day went.

## Be kind to yourself, too

If you feel teary leaving, you're in good company. It gets easier — usually within a week or two — as your child learns that daycare is a safe, fun place and that you always come back.
