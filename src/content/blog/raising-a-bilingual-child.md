Growing up with two languages is one of the greatest gifts you can give a young child — and it's easier to support than most parents think. At J&K, your child hears, sings, and plays in **both English and Spanish** every day. Here's how to keep both languages growing at home, whichever one you speak best.

## Why two languages is worth it

Before age five, children pick up sounds, rhythm, and grammar almost effortlessly. Bilingual kids tend to build strong listening skills, mental flexibility, and — just as important — a deeper connection to family and culture. And here's the reassuring part: **you don't need to be perfectly fluent in both languages to help.**

## Find a rhythm that fits your family

There's no single "right" way. Pick whatever feels natural and keep it consistent:

- **One parent, one language** — if each caregiver leans on a different language, stick with it. Children sort out who speaks what beautifully.
- **Or one place or time per language** — Spanish at dinner, English at bath time. Simple anchors work.
- **Consistency beats perfection.** A steady little bit every day does more than an occasional big push.

## Make it play, not a lesson

- **Read and sing in both.** Board books, nursery rhymes, and silly songs are language lessons in disguise.
- **Narrate everyday life** — "Vamos a lavar las manos," "Let's put on your shoes." Ordinary moments are the best practice.
- **Use the language you're strongest in.** Rich, warm, natural talk in one language beats stilted phrases in another.

## Don't worry about mixing

It's completely normal for bilingual children to blend both languages in a single sentence — you'll hear "I want más" and that's perfectly fine. This "code-switching" isn't confusion; it's a flexible brain choosing the fastest word it has. With time and exposure, they separate the two on their own.
