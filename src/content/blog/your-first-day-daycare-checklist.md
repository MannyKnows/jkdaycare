A little prep the night before takes a lot of stress out of the first morning. Here's a friendly checklist to get your little one ready for a smooth, happy start.

## What to pack

- **Spare clothes** (two full changes) — spills and puddles happen!
- **Diapers and wipes**, or extra underwear if you're potty-training.
- **A comfort item** — a favorite stuffed animal, blanket, or family photo.
- **Bottles, formula, or food**, clearly labeled, plus any feeding instructions.
- **A sun hat and weather-appropriate layers** for outdoor play.
- **Sunscreen** (with any consent forms your center needs).
- **A reusable water bottle**, if your child uses one.

## Label everything

Pop your child's name on **every** item — clothes, bottles, hats, and bags. It saves so much sorting at the end of the day and keeps belongings from going missing.

## The night before

- Lay out clothes and pack the bag together so it's not a morning scramble.
- Aim for an early bedtime — a well-rested child handles a big new day far better.
- Plan a calm, unhurried morning routine.

## The morning of

- Offer a good breakfast.
- Keep goodbyes warm and brief (see our post on [easing the first days](/blog/easing-the-first-days-at-daycare/)).
- Double-check your contact details and pickup plans with your center.

## A quick tip

Keep a small "restock" stash by the door — spare diapers, wipes, and a change of clothes — so refilling the bag each evening takes ten seconds.
