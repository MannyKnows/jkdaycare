Choosing where your child spends their days is a big decision, and a tour is your best chance to look beyond the brochure. Trust your instincts about how a place *feels* — and bring this list of questions to help you compare centers with confidence.

## Safety and health

- How do you handle sign-in, pickup, and door security?
- What are your cleaning, hand-washing, and illness policies?
- Are staff trained in pediatric first aid and CPR?
- How do you handle allergies and medication?

## Staff and care

- What is the **ratio** of caregivers to children, and how big is each group?
- How long have your teachers been here? (Low turnover is a great sign.)
- Who will be with my child each day, and what's their experience?
- How do you comfort a child who's upset or missing home?

## The daily routine

- What does a typical day look like — play, meals, naps, outdoor time?
- What's your approach to learning and to guiding behavior?
- How do you support different ages and developmental stages?

## Food, naps, and comfort

- Are meals and snacks provided, and how do you handle dietary needs?
- Where and how do children nap?
- Can my child bring a comfort item from home?

## Staying connected

- How will you keep me updated about my child's day?
- How do you handle questions or concerns from parents?

## Trust your gut

As you walk through, notice the little things: Are the children engaged and happy? Do caregivers get down to the children's level? Does it feel warm, calm, and clean? A place that welcomes your questions is a place that will welcome your child.
